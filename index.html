World War I

1914-1918

Event: A global conflict primarily between European powers, sparked by the assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria.
Significance: The war leads to major geopolitical changes, the collapse of empires (Austro-Hungarian, Ottoman, Russian, German), and the beginning of the 20th century’s political shifts.

World War II

1939-1945

Event: The deadliest conflict in history, involving most of the world’s nations, started by Nazi Germany’s invasion of Poland.
Significance: The war leads to the creation of the United Nations, the Cold War, the rise of the United States and the Soviet Union as superpowers, and the decolonization of Africa and Asia.
